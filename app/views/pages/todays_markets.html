Dow Jones

The Dow Jones industrial average is an index used by many as a way to measure the general health of the overall U.S. economy. The number often referred to in news reports and on finance websites is a scaled average of the stock price of 30 major U.S. companies.

Nasdaq Composite

The Nasdaq Composite, is an index profiling all of the companies listed on the NASDAQ stock exchange. This in turn makes an a very unique index, as it is only major index reported on in the U.S. that does not profile companies exclusively headquartered in the United States.

View details »

S&P 500

Standard & Poor's 500 is commonly through to be the best indicator of the U.S. economy. It is an aggregate of a select 500 U.S. based companies which are believed to be representative of the U.S. market. However, unlike the other indexes the S&P 500 uses a capitalization weighted index, meaning that places more emphasis on the stock prices of the larger companies.
